Common questions

What families ask first.

How much does senior home care cost in San Diego?
Hourly home care in San Diego averages $36–$42 per hour in 2026. Carevider's standard rate is $38/hr with a 4-hour minimum. Live-in care averages $385–$420/day; ours is $395/day. 24-hour shift care runs $28/hr with two caregivers. Final pricing depends on care level, location, and any specialty needs like dementia or hospice support.
How quickly can care start?
For most cases in San Diego County, we can start care within 24–48 hours of the initial call. For hospital discharges and crisis situations, same-day starts are common — we maintain a 24/7 intake line and on-call RN team specifically for this.
Will my long-term care insurance pay for this?
Yes, in most cases. We accept all major LTC insurance policies (Genworth, John Hancock, Mutual of Omaha, and others) and bill them directly so you don't pay out of pocket and wait for reimbursement. We also help VA-eligible families access the Aid & Attendance benefit.
What's the difference between home care and assisted living?
Home care brings caregivers to your loved one's home — they keep their environment, routine, and independence. Assisted living moves them to a residential community with on-site staff. We do both: home care directly, and placement assistance for assisted living and memory care communities at no cost to your family.
Are your caregivers employees or contractors?
All Carevider caregivers are W-2 employees — never independent contractors. They are background-checked, TB-tested, CPR-certified, and complete 40+ hours of training before their first shift. We carry full workers' compensation and liability insurance, so families are never liable for caregiver injury or tax obligations.

Ready to start, or just want to talk it through?

A free 30-minute call with one of our RNs. No sales script, no pressure. Just clarity on what your family needs next.
