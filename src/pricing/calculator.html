How the estimate works

The calculator multiplies your hourly rate by hours per day and days per week to get a weekly cost, then multiplies by 4.33 to project a month. It's the same math our team uses on the phone — just instant and private.

Weekly = rate × hours × days. Monthly = weekly × 4.33. Change any input and both numbers update immediately.

From estimate to exact price

An estimate is a great starting point, but the right care plan depends on details a calculator can't see — mobility, medications, cognition, home layout, and family schedules. That's what the free RN assessment is for.

A registered nurse reviews needs in person or by video, recommends a care level, and confirms your exact rate. If long-term care insurance or VA benefits apply, we verify coverage so your real out-of-pocket cost may be lower than the estimate shown here.

Common questions

Calculator FAQs

Is the calculator estimate a real quote?
No. The calculator gives a planning estimate based on hours, days, and care level using standard rates. Your exact price depends on specific care needs and schedule, and is confirmed during a free RN assessment. Benefits like LTC insurance or VA Aid and Attendance may lower your actual out-of-pocket cost.
Do I have to enter my contact information?
No. The calculator runs entirely in your browser and requires no name, email, or phone number. You can explore as many scenarios as you like privately. When you're ready to talk to a nurse, you choose to reach out — there's no obligation and the assessment itself is always free.
How is the monthly cost calculated?
The calculator multiplies your hourly rate by hours per day and days per week to produce a weekly total, then multiplies that by 4.33 — the average number of weeks in a month. This gives a realistic monthly projection rather than a simple four-week figure, which would understate most months.
Why do different care levels cost different amounts?
Higher care levels require more training and skill. Companion care covers company, errands, and light help; personal care adds bathing, dressing, and mobility support; specialized care covers dementia and complex needs. Each level carries a different hourly rate, which is why selecting a level changes your estimate.
Can benefits reduce the cost shown here?
Yes. The estimate reflects private-pay rates before any benefits. Long-term care insurance often reimburses non-medical home care, and eligible veterans may receive VA Aid and Attendance. We verify coverage and handle paperwork, so your real cost can be meaningfully lower than the calculator's figure.
