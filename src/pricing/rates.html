How to lower your cost

Most families reduce out-of-pocket cost by matching the care type to the real need. Live-in care is often far more affordable than 24-hour care when overnight needs are light. Adjusting hours and days also moves your total meaningfully — the calculator makes the trade-offs visible.

Benefits can cover a large share. Long-term care insurance frequently pays for non-medical home care, and eligible veterans or surviving spouses may qualify for VA Aid and Attendance. We verify coverage and handle the paperwork so you don't leave money on the table.

Want help mapping it out? A registered nurse will review needs and budget with you at no cost.

Rates & pricing FAQs

What is the hourly rate for home care in San Diego?
Hourly rates vary with care level, schedule, and total hours booked. Carevider quotes a clear hourly rate up front, with no hidden assessment or registration fees. The fastest way to see your number is our free calculator, followed by a no-cost RN assessment to confirm the exact rate for your plan.
What's the difference between live-in and 24-hour care?
Live-in care uses one caregiver who sleeps at the home and is billed as a daily rate, ideal for steady overnight needs. Twenty-four-hour care uses rotating awake caregivers for clients who need help around the clock. Live-in is usually more affordable; 24-hour suits higher-acuity needs. We help you choose.
Are there extra fees beyond the hourly rate?
No. Carevider's rate covers caregiver wages, payroll taxes, insurance, training, and supervision. We do not charge separate assessment, registration, or cancellation fees with reasonable notice. Mileage for errands may apply and is disclosed in advance. Everything is spelled out before care begins, so your bill holds no surprises.
Can I lower my home care costs?
Yes. Adjusting hours, choosing live-in over 24-hour care, and applying benefits all reduce out-of-pocket cost. Long-term care insurance and VA Aid and Attendance can cover a large share for those who qualify. We verify coverage and handle paperwork so you capture every dollar of benefit available.
Do you require a contract or deposit?
No long-term contract is required to begin care, and we don't lock families into lengthy commitments. You can adjust or pause your schedule as needs change. We'll explain any short-notice or holiday terms during your free assessment, so expectations are clear before the first shift starts.
